About me Hi, my name is Alexander Bays, and I’m a self taught programmer and part time graphic designer from Dallas, TX . I mainly focus on frontend design and functionality for both desktop and mobile software. I’ve created applications for Windows, macOS, iPhone, and Apple Watch. I would say my preference leans towards the Apple side, but I have no problem working on anything else inside of the frameworks and languages I have come to learn. Aside from learning Swift (and of course SwiftUI) early on to try my hand at Apple applications, I’ve implemented languages like C++ using the Qt library, which I REALLY love for desktop software, JavaScript/ HTML/ CSS inside of Electron and Tauri, C# for some Unity game programming, and Python for general scripting purposes and coding problems. Learning newer languages like Go and Rust are still goals as well. More: I started my self taught skills journey around 2010, teaching myself Photoshop and trying to be a graphic designer, but a little over 4 years ago (2018), I discovered programming through a computer engineer friend, and this of course became what I wanted to do. I was eventually pulled toward the design side of programming after being unsure what area I would focus on and what language(s) I would learn. I still want to become a talented programmer and build sofisticated software, but also to use my design insticts to make what I work on look good as well. These days my real focus is getting better at writing more complex and efficient code, and the design stuff is more for fun on the side, but I think it helps bring ideas for new programming projects as well.
